# Phase 1: Detailed Requirement Analysis & Market Research

## 1. Main Problem in Current Market

Most small businesses still manage inventory using:

* Excel sheets
* Manual notebooks
* WhatsApp messages
* Separate billing software
* Separate POS systems
* Separate accounting tools
* Different files for stock, sales, and purchases

This creates major problems:

* Duplicate entries
* Wrong stock quantity
* Missing purchase history
* Missing sales tracking
* No low-stock alerts
* No real-time visibility
* Difficult reporting
* Hard to manage multiple warehouses or branches
* Data spread across Excel, ERP, POS, and CRM tools
* Systems are often expensive, complicated, or not designed for small businesses ([Reddit][1])

Reddit discussions show that small businesses mainly struggle with:

* Overstocking
* Understocking
* Dead inventory
* Missing low stock alerts
* No vendor tracking
* Poor warehouse slotting
* Manual replenishment planning
* Lack of real-time inventory visibility ([Reddit][1])

## 2. Market Opportunity

The inventory management software market is growing rapidly. Businesses are moving from Excel and manual work to cloud-based inventory systems with automation, AI, and mobile access. The global inventory software market is projected to grow strongly over the next few years, driven by manufacturing, retail, warehouses, distributors, and SMEs. ([Fortune Business Insights][2])

Small businesses now want:

* Cloud-based systems
* Mobile-friendly dashboards
* Real-time stock tracking
* Barcode scanning
* Low stock alerts
* Reports and analytics
* Multi-warehouse support
* Easy setup without technical knowledge
* Affordable subscription plans
* AI-based demand forecasting and automation ([AT&T Newsroom][3])

There is also a growing demand for SaaS tools with built-in AI because businesses now expect software to automate repetitive work, provide recommendations, and reduce manual effort. AI-driven SaaS spending is increasing significantly in 2026. ([TechRadar][4])

## 3. Competitor Analysis

Some major competitors in the market are:

* Zoho Inventory

* SalesBinder
* QuickBooks Commerce
* Lightspeed
* Shopify POS
* Square

Most competitors offer:

* Product management
* Inventory tracking
* Sales and purchase modules
* Basic reporting
* Barcode support
* Multi-location support

But many still have limitations:

* Too expensive for small businesses
* Complex UI
* Limited customization
* Difficult onboarding
* Missing AI recommendations
* Weak dashboard customization
* Limited workflow automation
* Poor support for Indian GST/business requirements
* Separate tools required for CRM, accounting, POS, and warehouse management ([DOSS | Adaptive ERP and Operations Cloud][5])

## 5. User Personas

### Persona 1: Factory Owner

Needs:

* Raw material tracking
* Finished goods tracking
* Vendor management
* Production stock tracking
* Purchase order tracking

Pain Points:

* Cannot track stock properly
* Inventory mismatch
* Missing raw material alerts

### Persona 2: Retail Shop Owner

Needs:

* Product inventory
* Billing integration
* Sales tracking
* Customer management
* Barcode scanning

Pain Points:

* Manual Excel tracking
* No sales reports
* No stock alerts

### Persona 3: Warehouse Manager

Needs:

* Multi-location stock tracking
* Warehouse transfers
* Bin/rack tracking
* Inventory movement history

Pain Points:

* Wrong stock placement
* Missing products
* Difficult stock movement tracking

### Persona 4: Distributor

Needs:

* Purchase management
* Sales management
* Vendor tracking
* Route-based stock movement
* Invoice tracking

Pain Points:

* Delayed order fulfillment
* Lack of visibility across branches
* Poor vendor coordination

* Total products
* Total customers
* Total vendors
* Total stock value
* Low stock items
* Expired products
* Sales amount
* Purchase amount
* Profit / loss
* Top selling products
* Top customers
* Warehouse utilization
* Pending payments
* Recent activity logs
* Notifications

* Inventory movement chart
